(L.) Medik.

Malvaceae

Botanical References:

Nomenclature Notes:


Common Name:

Aibika

Range:

E. Asia - South-eastern Asia to Northern Australia.

Habitat:

Wasteland and humid rocky hillsides[260]. In Nepal it grows at elevations of 700 - 1700 metres in rocky places with shrubs[272]. Grasslands, near streams and margins of farm land[266].

General Information:

Aibika is a shrub growing up to 5 metres tall. The plant is often cultivated in the Tropics, especially in Asia and the Pacific Islands, both as an ornamental and for its edible leaves[200, 298, 300].

Known Hazards:

None known

Cultivation Details:

Easily grown in any well-drained soil in a sunny position[200]. Plants are most productive when grown in well-prepared fertile soils that are rich in organic matter[300]. Plants grow well in lowland tropical areas[300]. Yields begin to drop when plants are grown at elevations above 500 metres and plants may develop an annual habit of growth at higher elevations[300]. Plants have a high moisture requirement and grow best in areas with an evenly distributed annual precipitation of 1,000mm or more[300]. They prefer a relatively high humidity and a stable temperature above 25Â°c[300]. When well looked after, the plant can be highly productive - yields of 40 - 60 tonnes per hectare have been achieved[298, 300]. A very variable plant[300]. Plants do not flower well when day length is less than 12 hours[300]. Plants are somewhat susceptible to root nematodes[298].

Edible Uses:

Young leaves - raw or cooked[183, 200]. Sweet and mucilaginous[183, 300]. Young leaves can be used as a lettuce substitute[298]. Young shoots are harvested when about 15cm long[300]. Flower buds - raw or cooked[183].
In Nepal the root juice is warmed and applied to sprains[272]. The juice of the flowers is used to treat chronic bronchitis and toothache[272].

Medicinal:

The bark is said to be emmenagogue[240]. A paste of the bark is used to treat wounds and cuts, with new paste being applied every 2 - 3 days for about 3 weeks[272].

Propagation:

Seed - sow in situ. The seed should germinate with two weeks. Cuttings of young shoots.
